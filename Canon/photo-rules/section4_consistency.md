# SECTION 4 — VISUAL CONSISTENCY GUIDE

## 1. Era Rules (1958 U.S. South Alt-History)
Authentic mid-century amateur snapshot: Aged Kodachrome daylight (faded vibrancy, yellowed edges, slight color shift warmer), aged Ektachrome night/performance (desaturated reds/blues, minor dust specks/scratches). Grain prominent, warm highlights faded, blue shadows softened. Mimic 1958 photo found in grandmother's attic: a little old/faded, candid/not professionally set/lit, imperfect exposure/focus, natural moments. No modern lights/objects/clothes/effects. Per [[project-facts]] (Jim Crow, Elvis dead, no anachronisms like Broadcaster→Telecaster).

## 2. Camera & Lens
- 35mm default (snapshot style, slight curvature/vignette)  
- 50mm close-ups  
- No wide distortion, modern bokeh/depth  
- Grain heavy (low light intensified), possible minor motion blur/sharpness loss for candid feel

## 3. Lighting
**Allowed:** Amateur available light only – tungsten, neon, fair bulbs, sunlight, headlights, single red gumball (uneven pools, harsh contrasts, no fill).  
**Never:** LED/RGB/laser/bloom/lightbars, even studio correction.  
**Environments (faded attic print):**  
- Day: Faded warm Kodachrome, hazy/soft edges  
- Indoor day: Uneven window + dim tungsten, shadows deep  
- Night street: Deep indigo pools, faded red reflections  
- Warehouse: ONE overhead barn tungsten, hard uneven shadows  
- Juke/performance: Smoky low tungsten, hazy imperfect
