## Arcs

- [`Fandom & Flockening` ]: Love addiction, vision seeking, intimacy within fan-clubs - feeling of belonging,  common language and gestures. --> Actions call for coordinated groups, forming rules of engagement:  we are all zero days; working, motivating and leisure are all accepted behaviors; leaders and followers interchangeability, fluid interactions between members and groups: boids - dynamics between individual wills and a need to be part of something. quality of being a flocker: identifying as the sum of your relationships and belongings, be more about interactions than about achievements (such as deadlines)
- [`Romance`]: Tim and Alice’s romance begins as Alice’s aspirational adulthood play—claiming Tim to end Cindy/Eleanore feud, kissing him post-warehouse initiation—while Tim falls first, falsely claiming shared visions to bond as co-witnesses. It deepens into partnership through joint actions like Market Street luring and drive-in hand-pressing, where Alice universalizes intimacy while Tim clings to private claims, his aspirational lie curdling trust. Fractured by communal diffusion vs. individual possession, they split tragically: Alice chooses flock transmission, Tim solo authorship via Buzz.
- [`Timusician`]: Tim is a slightly bullied roadie for the band. Trying to impress Alice, he pawns his watch to buy a guitar and teaches himself to play. When the band discovers he wrote a song for Alice, his uncomfortable tutelage begins. Tim's song wins over Mr. MacAllister, who sends Evans. Evans starts a predatory relationship with Tim, promising him success as a capitalist musician, driving him away from the Flock and Evans. 
- [`UR`]: Mama Bettye Davis forms Steam Pillars as a UR tool for integration via shared joy and provocation. She shifts from band operator to face of a larger clandestine movement, until Buzz Music’s Mr. C. Evans lures them toward commercial recording and exposure. Sidelined by UR superiors for losing control, she’s absent from the train that take the band and flocks to their final destination.
- [`Alice`]: Coming of age in the age of coming of age
- ['Seeing']: The visions are termed "Seeing", like the Love. Some people experience them. Alice is fascinated and wants to talk about what she saw and what others saw. Elenore and her are interviewing people and write their visions for a fanzine. This evolves into a gathering proto-flock of visions telling after concerts. They sit around the fire and one time Babatunde joins them and drums. Alice feels how with his drumming the memories of visions become vivid. When two people have the same element in their vision there is joy about it, they give it a term (suggest: coseeing). This term later becomes a term for people sharing a vision also in more concrete way and is part of the flock jargon. The fan club is busy drawing the visions on cars and later as murals in East West Los Givataim.
  Meanwhile, Barry set up a fan club for people who didn't 'see', hiding the fact he actually had a vision. Veronika, who's spirituality is rooted in nature, joins in. They try to convince Pete to join, but he is cynical about it, until eventually he joins, and gives a rational explanation to why people have visions (dehydration and exhaustion) . Barry calls the fanclub "The Imagineers", claiming unlike visions coming from outside, they use their imagination. 
  Eventually Alice finds a way to make the distinction between seers and imagineers non hierarchical, in the spirit of zero day old.
