### Tim's Musical Arc
Tim is a very talented and attractive, very inexperienced, very impatient, very recognition hungry young man. 
- Talented... but inexperienced: he's good enough to teach himself to play a steam pillars song in a matter of a couple of weeks; after the band adopts him, he's able to get on stage as a background player in a matter of a few more weeks; by the time he's been playing for three months he's taking lead parts, and then even recording a single for a record company (Buzz Music.)  
- Recognition hungry... but attractive: Tim's one wish in his young life is to be recognized and credited as a man of usefulness and merit (he calls it "a real man.") However, it's clear to everyone including him that his success on stage is because of his animal magnetism. 
- Talented... but recognition hungry Tim writes a song for Alice's 18th birthday. This song will later get him a record deal which will alienate him from the Pillars and end his relationship with Alice. 
- Inexperienced... but recognition hungry. When the Steam Pillars co-opt his song, improving it dramatically, Tim feels robbed. When he hears Vilma will be singing his words and not him he's offended. 
- Inexperienced...  but impatient. Tim's song isn't all that good. It's a simple song. But it has three hit-making things going for it: it has a very simple, effective hook; it was worked on by the steam pillars; and it's backed by Tim's unmistakable presence on stage. The Steam Pillars advise him to wait until he gets better, and writes better songs. This is an unforgivable insult for him which drives him straight into the arms of Mr. C. Evans. 

#### Grounding Tim's musical arc
##### How come he gets good fast
- In side A, we focus on him "having all the records" -- that is, being very involved with music. 
- In side B, we learn what Tim does for a living and why he "has all the records." His stepdad services jukeboxes and cigarette machines across diners, bars, and clubs. Tim helps him, salvages retired and damaged 45s, and gradually builds a private library from route leftovers, swapped stock, and records adults treat as disposable. This may be hinted at in the jukebox game on [[Kick The Jukebox]]. 
- In side C, he teaches himself to play and sing along within two weeks (that is, in Columbia). He achieves that because Tim is obsessive: the way he watches Jo's fingers also extends to the fact he plays every night and doesn't stop until his fingers bleed. 
- When he reveals to the band he can play (see draft in playground: [[Tim reveals he can play guitar]]), we learn he has perfect pitch and good music knowledge. 
- After that Heath starts being his tutor, teaching him a workman's stagecraft: shortcuts and lessons learned, not theory. We don't get to experience that too often through Alice's eyes, but it noticeably improves him to the point he is brought on stage. 
##### Reason the steam pillars put him on
- He's not there to replace Heath or Vilma, he's more of a background player-singer. 
- By the time he gets to be on stage, the pillars have been changed from a five-piece band to "The Steam Pillars Collective", an ever-growing band that adds and sheds members every night.
##### Reasons the record company want him
- His song is called Real Honey, which happens to align well with Buzz music, the new record company's branding. 
- Mr. C. Evans explains to Tim Buzz is focused on raw talent over musical education, looking for a new Elvis -- someone with limited musical education, some talent, and raw stage charisma. "Music schools don't make music stars."
- It should be noted that Alice doesn't like the song and is embarrassed by it -- roughly for the same reasons Evans likes it.
